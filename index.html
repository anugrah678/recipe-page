Spaghetti alla
Carbonara

The Roman classic — silky eggs, crispy guanciale, no cream, ever.

Carbonara is Rome's greatest gift to the pasta world. Born in the Lazio kitchens of post-war Italy, this dish achieves something miraculous — a sauce that is simultaneously rich and light, built from nothing more than eggs, aged cheese, cured pork, and starchy pasta water. It demands attention, respect, and — above all — the absence of cream.

Ingredients

Serves 4 people. For best results, use room temperature eggs and freshly grated cheese — never pre-grated.

Pasta & Pork

  • 400 g Spaghetti or rigatoni bronze-die cut, if possible
  • 200 g Guanciale or pancetta as substitute

The Sauce

  • 4 large Egg yolks + 1 whole egg
  • 80 g Pecorino Romano finely grated
  • 40 g Parmigiano Reggiano finely grated
  • to taste Freshly cracked black pepper generous amount

For Cooking

  • 1 tbsp Coarse sea salt for pasta water

Method

⚠️ Chef's warning The #1 mistake is adding hot pasta to the egg mixture too fast. You'll get scrambled eggs. Let the pan cool off the heat for 30 seconds first, then toss vigorously while adding pasta water splash by splash.
  1. 1

    Boil the pasta water

    Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Salt it generously — it should taste like the sea. Add the spaghetti and cook until 2 minutes before the package's al dente time. Before draining, reserve at least 1 full cup of starchy pasta water.

  2. 2

    Render the guanciale

    Cut guanciale into 1cm lardons or strips. Place in a cold pan and turn heat to medium — starting cold renders the fat slowly without burning. Cook 8–10 minutes until golden and crispy. Remove from heat, keep the fat in the pan.

  3. 3

    Make the egg mixture

    In a bowl, whisk together 4 egg yolks and 1 whole egg. Add the grated Pecorino and Parmesan. Season heavily with black pepper. Whisk until thick and creamy. The consistency should be like a loose custard.

  4. 4

    Combine pasta and guanciale

    Drain the pasta (reserving your cup of pasta water). Toss it directly into the pan with the guanciale and rendered fat over medium-low heat for 1 minute, tossing continuously to coat.

  5. 5

    Create the silky sauce

    Remove the pan completely from the heat. Wait 30 seconds. Pour the egg mixture over the pasta. Toss vigorously and add pasta water tablespoon by tablespoon — the starch emulsifies the sauce into a glossy, creamy coating. Serve immediately on warm plates.

💡 Pro tip Carbonara waits for no one. Have warm bowls ready, your guests seated, and serve the moment it's done. It firms up fast and cannot be reheated.
